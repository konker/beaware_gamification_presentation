The BeAware Project

  • Joint European research project investigating how next generation ICT can be designed to reduce energy consumption in the home
  • 3 years 2008 - 2011
  • 8 partners in 3 countries: Italy, Sweden, Finland

Energylife

  • Sensors in the home measure the electricity usage of various devices
  • Data is transmitted and stored in a central data store
  • The Energylife iPhone application displays energy usage information in a meaningful way to the occupants of the home

Goals and Requirements

  • Encourage energy saving
  • Non-intrusive
  • Intuitive
  • Sustained involvement

What happened?

Results

  • 2 trials were conducted in households in Italy and Sweden
  • First trial provided feedback for refining the mechanics of the application

Results

  • Households decreased consumption by approximately 10% as compared to the previous year
  • Users increased their awareness and knowledge of energy conservation issues
  • Mixed feelings on the community aspect of the application

Results

  • Users liked receiving advice tips and quiz questions
  • Users generally liked the interface and the unobtrusive nature of the system
  • Users felt motivated to use the system in the future

Recap

  • Wanted to encourage sustained energy conservation
  • Incentivized engagement by attaching scores to activities which furthered these goals
  • Progressively incorporated richer functionalities
  • Added elements of (friendly) competition
  • System was well received and showed positive results
